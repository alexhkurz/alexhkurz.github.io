#### In California 

- Draught: The [snow drought](https://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/news/multi-century-evaluation-sierra-nevada-snowpack) in the Sierra Nevada is serious. It caused a [multi-year drought](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41612-018-0012-1), which in turn accelerated [falling groundwater levels](https://www.sciencemag.org/news/2020/04/droughts-exposed-california-s-thirst-groundwater-now-state-hopes-refill-its-aquifers), bringing us nearer to the end of agriculture as-we-know-it in the Central Valley, one of the most productive agricultural regions of the US. In the San Joaquin Valley, over the decades, [land surface has dropped](https://www.usgs.gov/mission-areas/water-resources/science/land-subsidence) by as much as 28 feet [due to groundwater pumping]((https://oehha.ca.gov/media/epic/downloads/droughttech2018.pdf)). Climate change [accelerates these trends](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1029/2018GL080308).

- Fires: [Millions of acres burned per year](https://twitter.com/dana1981/status/1335100427918929920/photo/1) 1987-2020. Map of the [perimeters of wildfires](https://projects.capradio.org/california-fire-history/#6/38.58/-121.49) up to 2018. [Hot and dry years](https://twitter.com/hausfath/status/1325958883576197121/photo/1)  of the last century. [UCI environmental engineers analyzed a century’s worth of the Golden State’s blazes](https://news.uci.edu/2021/04/22/californias-wildfire-season-has-lengthened-and-its-peak-is-now-earlier-in-the-year/).
