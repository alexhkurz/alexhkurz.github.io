# Coronavirus

On March 25, we heard for the first time that somebody died who we knew, a beloved [musician and violin teacher](https://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/news/leicester-news/plea-grieving-family-73-year-3977529) from Leicester.

On April 2, I heard that at the University of Birmingham, UK, 3 professors had died of the coronavirus.

[Ruth David](https://www.holocaust.org.uk/ruth-david-profile), whom we knew from the Leicester Quaker Meeting, and who had survived the Holocaust on the [Kindertransport](https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2018/nov/24/woman-saved-jewish-family-history-housekeeper) in 1939, sadly died of the coronavirus. [Obituary](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/jun/21/ruth-david-obituary).

[John Horton Conway](https://www.quantamagazine.org/john-conway-solved-mathematical-problems-with-his-bare-hands-20200420), one of the great mathematicians of our times, passed away.

[Peter Johnstone](https://www.joh.cam.ac.uk/my-tears-are-tears-joy-because-i-cannot-believe-how-lucky-i-am-have-survived-covid-19) (of topos theory fame) describes his brush with death in a moving piece.

## Policy Response

I am interested in how policy responses are shaped by a mixture of relying on models and on data. 

### early interventions

It seems to me that successful early responses relied on theory and models more than on data. They acted long before the data started to look serious. Most impressive are countries that acted early enough to avoid any lockdown. Others, such as Norway, acted later but nevertheless had entered lockdown even before their first death. Examples:

[Taiwan](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2762689), [Singapore](https://www.technologyreview.com/s/615353/singapore-is-the-model-for-how-to-handle-the-coronavirus/), Hong Kong, Japan, [Norway](http://www.tomdispatch.com/post/176698/tomgram%3A_ann_jones%2C_getting_trumped_by_covid-19/#more), [Kerala](https://www.indiatoday.in/magazine/cover-story/story/20200427-how-kerala-flattened-the-curve-1668033-2020-04-18)
