# Transition to a Sustainable Economy

Climate change is just one of the many problems we face, if we want human civilsation to survive the next, say, 500 years. To solve all the problems, it is clear that we need new ideas. 

It is not that there are no new ideas around, on the contrary, but they are not always easy to find in the mainstream media ... so I started to look around and make a list ... the fact that an idea makes the list does not mean that I endorse it, for example, I didn't study enough to have a firm opinion of the pros and cons of a job gurantee vs a basic universal income.

- **[Carbon Fee and Dividend](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_fee_and_dividend)**. One argument against taxes is that they put money in a pot that special interest groups and lobbyists will try to put their straws in. The carbon fee and dividend solves this problem by giving the money directly back to the tax payer. This allows us to internalise the soical costs of carbon by strengthening consumer power and not making government bigger. [The Conservative Case for Carbon Dividends](https://www.clcouncil.org/media/2017/03/The-Conservative-Case-for-Carbon-Dividends.pdf) also includes reducing regulation, shrinking government and promoting national security.

- **[Social Cost of Carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_cost_of_carbon).** Milton Copulos witnessed in a [Senate hearing](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CHRG-109shrg34739/html/CHRG-109shrg34739.htm) in 2006 that "the hidden cost of defense and other externalities came to [...] adding $3.68 to the price of a gallon of gasoline". And this number [does not account](http://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/viewdoc/download;jsessionid=F1D25490E7990535D5111AF030263D9A?doi=10.1.1.186.7523&rep=rep1&type=pdf) for climate change, biodiversity loss, our debts to future generations, etc. 

- **[Planetary Boundaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_boundaries)** is a field that studies the sweet spot of environmental parameters that supports human life on Earth. If we want to survive as a species we need to learn to live inside the planetary boundaries. This leads to the metaphor of a **[Doughnut Economy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rhcrbcg8HBw&feature=emb_logo)** that respects both the ceiling of planetary boundaries and the floor of our daily needs. Just recently, [Amsterdam](https://www.smartcitiesworld.net/news/news/amsterdam-adopts-first-city-doughnut-model-for-circular-economy-5198) adopted the doughnut economy as the basis for their covid 19 recovery plan.

- **[Poor People's Campaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poor_People%27s_Campaign:_A_National_Call_for_a_Moral_Revival)**. I believe that abolishing poverty is a prerequisite for staying inside planetary boundaries while maintaining democracy. [Liz Theoharis](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Es1j2XxgqGU&feature=emb_logo) takes a theological perspective and argues that abolishing poverty is as important to us as abolishing slavery was to the 18th and 19th century. Btw, [poverty is still rising](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/distribution-of-population-poverty-thresholds?country=~Sub-Saharan%20Africa) in parts of the world. [Social Costs of Poverty](https://www.poorpeoplescampaign.org/resource/costs-of-poverty-fact-sheet/).

- **[Modern Monetary Theory](https://stephaniekelton.com/book/)** answers the question of "How can we pay for this?". As long as there is both a demand for and availability of unused economic resources (eg if there are people who need more health care and if there are people who want to work as doctors as nurses) the lack of money cannot be the problem. MMT addresses this by explaining how fiat money works. A goverment is not a household, government spending is not restricted by income. Austerity is not an economic necessity but chosen economic politics. The blogs by  [Bill Mitchell](http://bilbo.economicoutlook.net/blog/) and [Richard Murphy](https://www.taxresearch.org.uk/Blog/) provide daily commentaries from this point of view.  I also find [Jason Hickel](https://www.jasonhickel.org/blog/2020/9/10/degrowth-and-mmt-a-thought-experiment) always worth reading.

- **[Green Amendments](https://forthegenerations.org/resources/)**. In order to secure a future, we need to recognize that we do not own the Earth, but are  caretakers for future generations. We need to rebalance our policital system so that the interests of future generations are represented. We need to amend the Constitution. A Green Amendment has been passed in [Pennsylvania](https://forthegenerations.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/FTG-Resources-GreenAmendmentFunctions.pdf) and a number of [countries](https://forthegenerations.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/Resources-SSRN-id1932779.pdf).

- **Quality of Life**. GDP was introduced to measure the performance of a war time economy. Tying economic success to the GDP today makes us blind for finding ways out of the current crisis. The GDP does not take into account unpaid work or environmental damage. Among the alternative proposals under discussion are the [the World Happiness Report](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Happiness_Report) and [Human Development Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index). 

- **[The American Anti-Corruption Act](https://anticorruptionact.org/)**. To get involved check out the bipartisan grassroot campaign [RepresentUS](https://represent.us/). They also made two hard hitting introductory videos: [Corruption is legal in the US](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5tu32CCA_Ig) and [How to Fix America's Corrupt Political System](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lhe286ky-9A). For a deeper study see Lawrence Lessig's book [Republic, Lost](https://republic.lessig.org/).  

- More ideas: [15 hours week](https://theconversation.com/its-time-to-put-the-15-hour-work-week-back-on-the-agenda-106754), [universal basic income](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_income), [negative income tax](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/negative-income-tax-explained), [job guarantee](http://pavlina-tcherneva.net/the-case-for-a-job-guarantee/), [zero waste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero_waste), [carbon tax](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_tax), [land value tax](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_value_tax), [permaculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Permaculture), [drug patents vs. prizes](https://www.drugpatentwatch.com/blog/driving-innovation-drug-patents-vs-prizes/), [Proposition 13](https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2019-08-14/california-proposition-13-business-taxes-split-roll), [steady state economy](https://steadystate.org/), plant based meat, [tiny home movement](https://ikeatinyhome.curbed.com/index.html), [degrowth](https://braveneweurope.com/jason-hickel-degrowth-a-response-to-branko-milanovic),  [private sufficiency/public luxury](https://www.monbiot.com/2019/09/20/embarrassment-of-riches/), [limitarianism](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/19452829.2019.1633734), [ecocide](https://www.theguardian.com/law/2020/nov/30/international-lawyers-draft-plan-to-criminalise-ecosystem-destruction), [progressive originalism](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j3XKx8e1ZU0), [ecosystem services](https://www.robertcostanza.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/2017_J_Costanza-et-al.-20yrs.-EcoServices.pdf), [capital as power](https://capitalaspower.com/blog/), [Four-day week](https://www.scotsman.com/news/politics/four-day-week-scotlands-public-sector-could-create-60000-jobs-report-says-3055185), ... to be continued ...
